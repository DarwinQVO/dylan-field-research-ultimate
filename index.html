Early Life & Mathematical Prodigy

Dylan Field was born on October 18, 1992, in Penngrove, California, a small town in Sonoma County. His parents were working-class professionals: his father worked as a respiratory therapist at Santa Rosa Memorial Hospital, and his mother as a resource specialist teacher at Thomas Page Elementary School.

The Famous "Chocolate" Essay

One of the most memorable aspects of Field's application was his response to the question "Tell us one thing about the world that you strongly believe is true, but that most people think is not true."

Chocolate is repulsive. Even the smell of it makes me want to vomit.

Dylan Field's contrarian take in his Thiel Fellowship application

Source: How I Built This - Dylan Field

"The application asked candidates what they thought most other people got wrong, and Dylan wrote, 'Chocolate is repulsive,' then launched into an essay backing up his opinion"

Field elaborated that according to baby videos, he never enjoyed chocolate. His first experience with sweets was on his first birthday with a chocolate cake, and footage showed his "face changes from joy to confusion to visible disgust." This "meta-contrarian" essay helped him stand out among applicants.

The Pivot to Design Tools

The breakthrough came when they noticed Adobe Fireworks was being discontinued. They formed the thesis that "as software gets easier to build, design becomes more important." Combined with Field's frustration using Fireworks during his Flipboard internship, they decided to create a collaborative, browser-based design tool using WebGL.

Source: Adobe Announcement 2012

The Building Years (2012-2015)

The early years of Figma were marked by technical challenges, leadership growing pains, and a patient approach to product development that ultimately proved crucial to the company's success.

Technical Challenges

Building a browser-based design tool using WebGL was extraordinarily difficult. Wallace developed a hybrid C++/JavaScript architecture that made it possible to build a best-in-class design tool in the browser, but the technical complexity meant development took much longer than anticipated.

Source: The Verge Technical Analysis

Technical challenges of building browser-based design tools using WebGL technology in 2012-2015.

Funding Challenges

During 2013-2014, when meeting with investors, Field admits "the company wasn't clear about what product it was building and what problems it would solve." This led to multiple rejections.

Community Building Strategy

Field personally coded a script to break down different clusters within the design community on Twitter, identifying the most influential people within sub-groups like typographers, iconographers, illustrators, and product designers. Figma's key differentiators included:

  • Developer handoff: Easier transition from design to code

The Failed Acquisition (2022-2023)

The attempted acquisition of Figma by Adobe for $20 billion represents one of the most significant deals in design software history and its ultimate failure reshaped both companies' futures.

The Deal Announcement

September 15, 2022: Adobe announced plans to acquire Figma for $20 billion, making it one of the largest software acquisitions in history.

Source: Adobe Official Announcement
